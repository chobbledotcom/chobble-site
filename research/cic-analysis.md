# Should Chobble register as a CIC (Community Interest Company)?

## Summary

Chobble's values and operating principles align strongly with the CIC model.
The main practical benefits would be limited liability (if currently a sole
trader), credibility with charity/community clients, and legal protection of
the social mission. The main drawbacks are no tax advantages (unlike
charities), additional administrative overhead, and reduced flexibility due to
the permanent asset lock.

## Why Chobble fits the CIC model

- Already operates with community benefit principles (discounted rates for
  charities, cooperatives, artists, vegan/renewable businesses)
- Donates 10% of income to charity
- Releases all code as open source (informal asset lock already exists)
- Refuses work from harmful industries
- Transparency and fair pricing are core principles
- Philosophical foundation (utilitarianism, anarchism) aligns with social
  enterprise ethos

## Benefits of CIC status

### Credibility signal
Many charity/community clients recognise and trust CICs. The "CIC" suffix
provides instant recognition that this is not a standard profit-maximising
company.

### Access to social enterprise funding
Some grants, social investment, and local authority contracts are restricted to
CICs, charities, or recognised social enterprises.

### Legal mission protection
The asset lock ensures the community-focused mission survives changes in
ownership, partnership, or management. Values are baked into the legal
structure rather than being a personal choice.

### Founder can still be paid
Unlike charities, CIC directors can draw market-rate salaries and retain
strategic control.

### Lighter governance than a charity
Less oversight than the Charity Commission. Annual Community Interest Report
(form CIC34) required, but simpler than charity governance.

### Limited liability
If currently operating as a sole trader, a CIC provides personal asset
protection.

## Drawbacks of CIC status

### No tax advantages
CICs pay corporation tax like any normal company. No gift aid, business rate
relief, or income/capital gains exemptions (those are charity-only benefits).

### Administrative overhead
Annual accounts, confirmation statement, and Community Interest Report filings
required. More paperwork than a sole trader.

### Asset lock is permanent
Cannot convert back to an ordinary company. Dividends capped at 35% of
distributable profits (if limited by shares). Must dissolve the CIC if
circumstances change.

### Less public recognition than "charity"
Many people don't know what a CIC is. Charities carry more brand recognition.

### Limited funding advantage for consultancies
Most CIC-specific grants fund community projects rather than ongoing service
businesses. The funding advantage may be smaller than expected in practice.

### Already operating this way voluntarily
Formalising values that already exist adds legal constraints without
necessarily changing behaviour.

## Alternatives to consider

### Charitable Incorporated Organisation (CIO)
- Tax relief benefits (gift aid, business rate relief, income/capital gains
  exemptions)
- Much heavier governance and Charity Commission oversight
- Restrictions on paying yourself as director/trustee
- Stronger public brand recognition

### Standard Limited Company
- Maximum flexibility
- No mission lock or asset lock
- No social enterprise credibility signal
- Standard corporation tax treatment

### Cooperative (Co-op)
- Democratic governance structure
- Aligns with anarchist/anti-hierarchical values
- Can be registered with the FCA
- Could qualify for some social enterprise funding

## Recommendation

If currently a sole trader: registering as a CIC is worth doing for limited
liability and the credibility signal to community clients.

If already a limited company: the main benefit is credibility and mission
lock, at the cost of reduced flexibility and no tax benefit.

If tax relief is important: consider a CIO instead, but expect heavier
governance.

The strongest argument: Chobble already operates like a CIC. Making it
official costs relatively little and signals commitment to exactly the kind of
clients Chobble wants to serve.
